Figure 1b. Acute pericarditis due to coxsackie B virus infection in a 54-year-old man. **(a)** Four-chamber double inversion-recovery black blood MR image shows circumferential pericardial thickening (arrows). **(b)** Short-axis late gadolinium-enhanced MR image shows diffuse circumferential late contrast enhancement (arrows) of the pericardium, a finding consistent with pericardial inflammation.

Figure 2a. **(a)** Illustration of short-axis plane of the heart shows bowing of the ventricular septum toward the left (arrow) in inspiration, which indicates exaggerated ventricular interdependence, a feature of pericardial constriction. *LV* = left ventricle, *RV* = right ventricle. **(b)** Image from short-axis real-time cine imaging in a 70-year-old man shows inspiratory septal flattening (arrow), which indicates exaggerated ventricular interdependence, a highly specific feature of pericardial constriction.
