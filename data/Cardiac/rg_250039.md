Figure 2. Radiographic assessment of malpositioned leads. **(A)** Posteroanterior chest radiograph shows a lead in the right atrial appendage (RAA) (arrowhead) but an atypical superior course and location of a right ventricle (RV) lead (arrow). **(B)** Lateral chest radiograph shows the appropriate location of the RAA lead (arrowhead) but an abnormal location of the RV lead, which is positioned in the left ventricle (arrow) (confirmed at echocardiography). **(C)** Axial chest CT image in the same patient after removal of the abnormally positioned RV lead shows a large atrial septal defect (arrow), which resulted in the aberrant course and location of the ventricular lead.
