![](/cms/10.1148/rg.250117/asset/images/rg.250117.fig2.jpg)

Figure 2. Clinically significant postbiopsy bleeding in a 48-year-old otherwise healthy woman who underwent stereotactic biopsy for grouped coarse heterogeneous calcifications at the 10-o’clock position in the right breast (Breast Imaging and Reporting Data System [BI-RADS] category 4B). Despite surgical evacuation of an expanding hematoma at the biopsy site, the patient continued to experience bleeding. Further evaluation with CT angiography identified a 10-mm pseudoaneurysm of the prepectoral branch of the thoracoacromial artery. Color Doppler US image of the biopsy site shows the bidirectional (yin-yang) Doppler flow pattern of the pseudoaneurysm. The pseudoaneurysm was eventually treated with thrombin injection.
