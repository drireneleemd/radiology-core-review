Figure 2. Abscess of the prostate and seminal vesicles, left funiculitis, and epididymo-orchitis in a 54-year-old man who presented with bilateral groin pain. On-call CT showed an abscess replacing the prostate. MRI was performed to delineate the extent of infection and exclude underlying malignancy. **(A)** Axial T2-weighted MR image shows the abscess replacing the bilateral seminal vesicles (solid arrow) and thickening of the left spermatic cord (dotted arrow). **(B)** Coronal T2-weighted MR image shows thickening of the left spermatic cord (white solid arrow), bulky left epididymis (white dotted arrow), and loss of normal T2 hyperintensity in the left testis (black arrow) relative to the right testis. The patient was treated with CT-guided transgluteal pigtail drainage of the abscess and antibiotics.
