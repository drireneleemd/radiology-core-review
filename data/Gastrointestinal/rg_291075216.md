**Figure 2.**  Ménétrier disease. Axial contrast-enhanced CT image of the distal stomach reveals that the body is primarily involved and the antrum is relatively spared (arrows).

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.291075216/asset/images/g09ja16g03x.jpeg)

**Figure 3.**  Fluoroscopic barium upper gastrointestinal spot view of the stomach demonstrates marked wall thickening, primarily in the fundus and body.

* [Download as PowerPoint](/action/downloadFigures?doi=10.1148%2Frg.291075216&id=F3)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.291075216/asset/images/g09ja16c04x.jpeg)

**Figure 4.**  Endoscopic image of the body of the stomach depicts severely hypertrophic and edematous rugal folds with irregularity of the gastric mucosa.

* [Download as PowerPoint](/action/downloadFigures?doi=10.1148%2Frg.291075216&id=F4)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.291075216/asset/images/g09ja16c05x.jpeg)

**Figure 5.**  Photomicrograph (original magnification, ×4; hematoxylin-eosin stain) of a specimen taken from the gastric antrum shows gastric mucosa with elongated and tortuous pits (curved arrows) and cystic dilatation (straight arrow).

* [Download as PowerPoint](/action/downloadFigures?doi=10.1148%2Frg.291075216&id=F5)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.291075216/asset/images/g09ja16c06x.jpeg)

**Figure 6.**  Photomicrograph (original magnification, ×4; hematoxylin-eosin stain) of a specimen obtained during a full-thickness biopsy of gastric mucosa shows foveolar hyperplasia (straight arrow), elongated pits (jagged arrow), glandular cystic dilatation (curved arrow), and focal acute and chronic inflammation with eosinophils.
