## Figures

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.250154/asset/images/rg.250154.fig1.jpg)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.250154/asset/images/rg.250154.fig2.jpg)

Figure 2. Esophagram in an aspirating patient. Left posterior oblique esophagram shows that the patient aspirated contrast agent in the trachea (white arrows). A study should not be immediately aborted due to aspiration and should instead proceed with caution, and only a small amount of contrast agent should be used. There may be a structural problem causing or contributing to the aspiration. In this case, aspiration was occurring in the setting of a partially obstructing mass (black arrows) in the cervical and upper thoracic esophagus. Pathologic findings revealed a squamous cell neoplasm.
