## Figures

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.285075160/asset/images/g08se04g01x.jpeg)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.285075160/asset/images/g08se04g02x.jpeg)

**Figure 2.**  Axial image from a multiplanar CT study shows the vermiform appendix, which contains homogeneous high-attenuation fluid (up to 30 HU) and proteins. Part of the lesion is protruding, balloonlike, into the lumen of the cecum. There is no peripheral wall calcification, desmoplastic reaction, fat infiltration, or lymphadenopathy. *bc* = base of the cecum, *dub* = dome of the urinary bladder, *m* = mucocele.

* [Download as PowerPoint](/action/downloadFigures?doi=10.1148%2Frg.285075160&id=F2)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.285075160/asset/images/g08se04g03x.jpeg)

**Figure 3.**  Coronal image from a multiplanar CT study illustrates the hourglass configuration of the mucocele. *bc* = base of the cecum, *m* = mucocele.

* [Download as PowerPoint](/action/downloadFigures?doi=10.1148%2Frg.285075160&id=F3)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.285075160/asset/images/g08se04c04x.jpeg)

**Figure 4.**  Photograph of the tumor specimen shows that the preoperative diagnosis of a mucocele was correct. Note that the lumen is filled with mucus.

* [Download as PowerPoint](/action/downloadFigures?doi=10.1148%2Frg.285075160&id=F4)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.285075160/asset/images/g08se04c05x.jpeg)

**Figure 5.**  Photomicrographs (original magnification, ×25; hematoxylin-eosin stain in **5** and Alcian blue-PAS stain in **6)** of the mucocele demonstrate papillary cylindrical epithelium, mucin production, and formation of papillae. There is no evidence for progression of the mass to carcinoma.
