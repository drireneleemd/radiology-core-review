**Figure 2.**  CT scout image (obtained with the patient supine) shows a round, gas-filled mass (arrow) in the pelvis adjacent to the sigmoid colon.

* [Download as PowerPoint](/action/downloadFigures?doi=10.1148%2Frg.266065019&id=F2)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.266065019/asset/images/g06nv14g03x.jpeg)

**Figure 3.**  Axial unenhanced CT scan of the abdomen shows a 9 × 9-cm cystic mass with an air-fluid level (arrowhead). The mass has a thickened wall and is in continuity with the sigmoid colon. Inflammation of the surrounding mesentery is seen.

* [Download as PowerPoint](/action/downloadFigures?doi=10.1148%2Frg.266065019&id=F3)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.266065019/asset/images/g06nv14g04x.jpeg)

**Figure 4.**  Image from a contrast enema study demonstrates a mass (\*) with a smooth wall that fills with contrast material from the sigmoid colon. The sigmoid colon has numerous diverticula (arrowheads).

* [Download as PowerPoint](/action/downloadFigures?doi=10.1148%2Frg.266065019&id=F4)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.266065019/asset/images/g06nv14c05a.jpeg)

**Figure 5a.**  **(a)** Photograph of the resected colonic segment shows a large mass arising from the segment. Scale is in centimeters. **(b)** Photograph of the sectioned surgical specimen shows that the mass is extraluminal, with a connection to the colon (arrowhead). The diverticulum contained fecal material.

* [Download as PowerPoint](/action/downloadFigures?doi=10.1148%2Frg.266065019&id=F5A)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.266065019/asset/images/g06nv14c05b.jpeg)

**Figure 5b.**  **(a)** Photograph of the resected colonic segment shows a large mass arising from the segment. Scale is in centimeters. **(b)** Photograph of the sectioned surgical specimen shows that the mass is extraluminal, with a connection to the colon (arrowhead). The diverticulum contained fecal material.

* [Download as PowerPoint](/action/downloadFigures?doi=10.1148%2Frg.266065019&id=F5B)

![](/cms/10.1148/rg.266065019/asset/images/g06nv14c06x.jpeg)

**Figure 6.**  Photomicrograph (original magnification, × 10; hematoxylineosin stain) shows colonic mucosa (arrowheads) in the wall of the diverticulum and no smooth muscular layer.
